**Royal Decree 933/2021** sets the documentary registration and reporting obligations for individuals and legal entities carrying out lodging activities in Spain.

If you manage a tourist accommodation —hotel, apartment, rural house, or any regulated lodging— this rule applies to you directly.

## Royal Decree 933/2021: who has to comply

The decree was published in 2021. The obligation to **submit electronically** through SES.HOSPEDAJES came into force in **December 2024**.

It applies to every individual or legal entity providing lodging services. The practical list:

- Hotels, hostels, guesthouses.
- Tourist apartments and tourist-use dwellings (VUT).
- Rural houses, youth hostels, mountain refuges, campgrounds.
- Private vacation rentals on platforms (Airbnb, Booking, VRBO, etc.) when offered as tourist lodging.
- Seasonal rentals with lodging services.

Does it cover pure seasonal rental without lodging services? The current administrative reading is yes when there's tourist turnover. The boundary is decided case by case. If in doubt, check with your advisor.

## Spain RD 933/2021 vacation rental obligations in plain English

Since December 2024, every accommodation must:

1. **Collect the data of every traveller** staying at the property.
2. **Submit a guest report** to the Spanish Ministry of the Interior through the SES.HOSPEDAJES platform.
3. **Report the lodging reservation** with the details of the stay (dates, number of guests, type of accommodation).

Electronically. The old paper reports are no longer accepted.

## Royal Decree 933 Spain: what data must be collected

The rule requires, as a minimum:

- **Full name** (first name, first surname, and second surname for Spanish documents).
- **Sex and date of birth**.
- **Nationality**.
- **Type and number of ID document** (DNI, NIE, passport, or ID document from the country of origin).
- **Support number** (required for the Spanish DNI).
- **Usual residential address**.
- **Contact phone and/or email**.
- **Relationship** to an accompanying adult, for minors aged 14 to 17.

For the **lodging reservation**, the contract details are added. In some cases, **payment data** as well: type (card, bank transfer, cash), holder, last four digits if a card. Whether payment data is mandatory depends on the type of lodging and the length of the stay. For tourist apartments, the current administrative reading is that payment data is included.

Minors under 14 are exempt from individual registration, though they must be listed as companions. We go deeper into how the rule applies to children and teenagers in [Minors in guest registration](/en/blog/minors-guest-registration/).

## Spain guest data law 933/2021: deadline for submission

The rule asks for the communication **at the moment of check-in** or within the **24 hours** following the start of the stay.

In practice:

- The **guest report** is sent to the Ministry within the first hours of the stay.
- The **lodging reservation** can be sent earlier (when the booking is confirmed) or together with the guest report.
- A **cancellation** is reported if the reservation had already been submitted and the guest doesn't arrive.

Sending a report a week later is an untimely communication. Sanctionable.

## How long do I keep the data?

**Three years.**

After that period, data must be deleted or anonymised. The retention is fixed by the rule. Not discretionary. Keeping it longer without an additional legal basis would breach GDPR.

Your software should purge automatically. Trusting yourself to do it by hand is the same as not doing it.

## What is SES.HOSPEDAJES?

**SES.HOSPEDAJES** is the Spanish Ministry of the Interior's platform, run by the Secretary of State for Security. It's where you submit guest reports and lodging reservations.

The submission process is complex. Not practical to do by hand. Most accommodations need software that automates the generation and submission of the reports.

To access SES.HOSPEDAJES you need:

- A **lessor code** assigned by the system at registration.
- An **establishment code** for each property.
- **Access credentials** (SOAP username and password) for automatic submission.

We explain how to request each in [How to obtain SES.HOSPEDAJES credentials step by step](/en/blog/ses-hospedajes-credentials/).

## What is sent to the Ministry

Each submission includes two communications:

- **Guest report**: the personal data of each registered guest.
- **Lodging reservation**: check-in and check-out dates, number of guests, type of contract, accommodation details.

Both are sent together. Once processed, you receive a confirmation of acceptance or, if there are errors, the detail to fix them.

## RD 933/2021 and payment card data: is it mandatory?

A question that comes up in every conversation with owners.

The rule includes payment-method data among the fields required for the lodging reservation. Yes. So you have to collect:

- **Type** of payment method (card, bank transfer, cash, etc.).
- **Holder** of the means.
- **Identification** of the means (last four digits in the case of a card).

You don't collect every digit of the card. You don't collect the CVV or the expiry date. Just enough to identify the means. Collecting more would be a GDPR minimisation issue.

## What happens if I don't comply?

Failing to comply can lead to **administrative penalties**.

The amount depends on severity and repetition. Falls under public-safety legislation (Organic Law 4/2015). Amounts range from €100 (minor) to €600,000 (very serious). We break it down in [Penalties for non-compliance with RD 933/2021](/en/blog/royal-decree-933-2021-penalties/).

Beyond the fines, proper guest registration is the responsibility of every tourist accommodation operator.

## Which autonomous communities have their own regulations?

Most autonomous communities use the Ministry's SES.HOSPEDAJES. Two exceptions:

- **Catalonia**: reporting goes to the **Mossos d'Esquadra** through the Generalitat's system.
- **Basque Country**: reporting goes to the **Ertzaintza** through the Basque Government's system.

If your property is in either, you'll need the regional system. We dig into the why and how in [Guest registration in Catalonia and the Basque Country](/en/blog/catalonia-basque-country-guest-registration/).
