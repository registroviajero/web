**Royal Decree 933/2021** establishes the documentary registration and reporting obligations for natural persons or legal entities that carry out lodging activities in Spain. If you manage a tourist accommodation — whether a hotel, apartment, rural house, or any type of regulated lodging — this regulation applies directly to you.

## What does the regulation require?

Since the obligation to submit electronically came into force in **December 2024**, all lodging establishments must:

1. **Collect the data of every traveler** staying at their premises.
2. **Submit a guest report** to the Spanish Ministry of the Interior through the SES.HOSPEDAJES platform.
3. **Report the lodging reservation** with the details of the stay (dates, number of guests, type of accommodation).

All of this must be done electronically — the old paper reports are no longer accepted.

## What data must be collected from each guest?

The regulation requires, as a minimum:

- **Full name** (first name, first surname, and second surname for Spanish documents)
- **Sex and date of birth**
- **Nationality**
- **Type and number of ID document** (DNI, NIE, passport, or ID document from the country of origin)
- **Support number** (required for Spanish DNI)
- **Usual residential address**
- **Contact phone and/or email**
- **Relationship** to an accompanying adult, for minors aged 14 to 17

Minors under 14 are exempt from individual registration, though they must be listed as companions. We go deeper into how the rule applies to children and teenagers in [Minors in guest registration](/en/blog/minors-guest-registration/).

## What is SES.HOSPEDAJES?

**SES.HOSPEDAJES** is the Spanish Ministry of the Interior's platform (managed by the Secretary of State for Security) to which accommodations must submit guest reports and lodging reservations.

The submission process is complex and not practical to do manually — most accommodations need software that automates the generation and submission of the reports.

To access SES.HOSPEDAJES you need:

- A **lessor code** provided by the authorities.
- An **establishment code** for each property.
- **Access credentials** (username and password) provided by the Policía Nacional or the Guardia Civil.

We explain how to request each of these in the guide [How to obtain SES.HOSPEDAJES credentials step by step](/en/blog/ses-hospedajes-credentials/).

## What is sent to the Ministry?

Each submission includes two communications:

- **Guest report**: the personal data of each registered guest.
- **Lodging reservation**: check-in and check-out dates, number of guests, type of contract, and accommodation details.

Both are sent together to the Ministry. Once processed, you receive a confirmation of whether the communication was accepted or contains errors you need to correct.

## What happens if I don't comply?

Failing to comply with the obligations under RD 933/2021 can lead to **administrative penalties**. The amount depends on the severity and repetition of the infraction, but it falls under public safety legislation. Amounts start at €601 for minor infractions and can escalate into several thousand — we break down the brackets and scenarios in [Penalties for non-compliance with RD 933/2021](/en/blog/royal-decree-933-2021-penalties/).

Beyond penalties, proper guest registration is a legal obligation that contributes to public safety and is the responsibility of every tourist accommodation operator.

## Which autonomous communities have their own regulations?

Most autonomous communities use the Ministry of the Interior's SES.HOSPEDAJES. There are, however, exceptions:

- **Catalonia**: accommodations must report the data to the **Mossos d'Esquadra** through their own system.
- **The Basque Country**: reporting is done to the **Ertzaintza**.

If your accommodation is in either of these communities, you will need to use the specific systems of each regional police force.
